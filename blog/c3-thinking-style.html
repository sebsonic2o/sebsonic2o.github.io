My Thinking Style
Or thinking Concrete and Random Sequential...

Sure, we are all unique and a category does not define who we are. This does not mean that attempts to categorize our mental constructs have no value. For instance, it helps being more aware of our own predispositions. Predispositions are neither good nor bad. So why would we need to be more aware of them? Because they can become a detriment to our life if we let them be arbitrary tendencies, preventing us from acting according to conditions.

One of those attempts is the Gregorc's mind style model. I took the test here, and I scored equally high on Concrete Sequential and Abstract Sequential.

According to Gregorc's model, I would learn best when:
In a structured and stimulating environment,
Facing predictable situations,
Relying on others to complete a task,
Working alone.

On the other hand, I would find it hard when:
In an unorganized environment,
Following incomplete or unclear directions,
With no predictable outcome,
With too little time to deal with a subject thoroughly,
Working in groups,
Expressing my emotions,
Being diplomatic to convince others,
Not monopolizing a conversation.

This makes me more aware that I try very hard to have control over everything I do. When working or pairing with others, I now realize that I use a lot of energy to be in control in very subtle ways. As a result, I feel nervous before and drained after. I intend to be more willing to truly relinquish control, and still be engaged. Thanks, Anthony Gregorc!
